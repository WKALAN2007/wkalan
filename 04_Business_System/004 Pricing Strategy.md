# 004 — Pricing Strategy

## Philosophy

**Price communicates value. Underpricing lies to the client about what they're getting.**

HK$500 is not cheap for a student. It's an investment. Someone paying HK$500 for a digital identity is saying: *"Being understood matters to me more than a new pair of shoes."*

That's the right client.

## Why These Prices?

### They're accessible but not cheap.
A student can save HK$500. A working creator can invest HK$2,000. Neither price says "bargain bin." Both say: *"This is worth something."*

### They scale with volume.
At 50 clients/year:
- 25 Sample ($500) = HK$12,500
- 15 Advance ($1,000) = HK$15,000
- 10 Pro ($2,000) = HK$20,000
- **Total: HK$47,500/year** (approx. US$6,100)

This is Year 1 — building the portfolio, proving the process.

### They leave room to grow.
When WKALAN has 10 case studies and a reputation, prices increase:

| Tier | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|------|--------|--------|--------|
| Sample | $500 | $800 | $1,200 |
| Advance | $1,000 | $1,500 | $2,500 |
| Pro | $2,000 | $3,000 | $5,000 |

---

## Pricing Psychology

### 1. Three tiers — no more, no less
Three options is the sweet spot. Too few: no choice. Too many: decision paralysis. Three: good/better/best — clear, easy, confident.

### 2. The middle tier is the target
Advance (HK$1,000) should feel like the obvious choice. It has most of the Pro value at half the price. Sample is the gateway. Pro is the aspiration.

### 3. Never discount
Discounts devalue the work and punish clients who paid full price. Instead of discounting, offer a "student rate" or "first-client rate" as a separate, transparent tier — not a markdown.

### 4. Payment structure: 50/50
50% upfront to book the slot. 50% before launch to go live. No surprises. No chasing invoices. Professional from day one.
