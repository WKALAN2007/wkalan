# 019 — Client Experience

## Every Client Should Feel Three Things

1. **Understood.** "They got me in a way nobody else has."
2. **Safe.** "I trust them with my story."
3. **Proud.** "This is me. Finally."

---

## Experience Principles

### 1. First response within 24 hours
A fast first reply sets the tone. Even if it's just: "Got your message. I'll respond properly tomorrow."

### 2. Never make them repeat themselves
Everything they tell you goes into their project file. Their story. Their preferences. Their fears. If they told you in the interview, you remember it.

### 3. Over-communicate during build
Daily update. Screenshot or preview link. "Here's what I worked on today." Silence breeds anxiety.

### 4. Ask permission, not forgiveness
Before using their story in a case study. Before sharing their site publicly. Before trying something unconventional in the design. Consent is part of the experience.

### 5. The final delivery is not a URL
It's a moment. When they open their site and see themselves — some cry. Some call their mom. Some just sit there, quiet.

That moment is the product.

## Touchpoints Map

```
First DM/Email
  → Response within 24hrs. Warm. Human. No template-speak.

Discovery Call Booking
  → Calendly link. Confirmation email. "Looking forward to meeting you."

Pre-Interview
  → "No need to prepare anything. Just bring yourself. This is a conversation, not a test."

During Interview
  → Recorded (with permission). Questions from the framework. Follow the tangents.

Post-Interview
  → "Thank you. I heard things today that will shape everything we build."

Design Direction Presentation
  → "Here's what I heard. Here's what it means visually. Tell me if I got it right."

Daily Build Updates
  → Screenshot or link. "Today I worked on the timeline section. Here's how your journey looks."

Review
  → One batch of feedback. Responded to within 48 hours. "Here's everything I changed based on your notes."

Launch Day
  → "It's live. This is you, online, for the world to meet."

One Week After
  → "How's it feeling? Anything need adjusting?"

One Month After
  → "Checking in. How are people responding to your site?"

Case Study Request
  → "Would you be open to us telling your story? No pressure. Only if it feels right."
```
