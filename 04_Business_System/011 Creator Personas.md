# 011 — Creator Personas

## Every Creator Is Different. Here Are the Patterns.

These personas help us understand different creators faster. They're starting points — not stereotypes. Every interview reveals the person beyond the persona.

---

## The Six Creator Types

### 1. The Storyteller (教育型創作者)
*Makes: Essays, video essays, documentaries, educational content*
- **Identity need:** Their content is deep. Their online presence should match.
- **Common pain:** "People know my analysis. Nobody knows why I care about this topic."
- **Design direction:** Editorial. Typography-led. Long-form reading spaces. Serif-forward.
- **Example sections:** Philosophy / Reading List / Behind the Research
- **Best tier:** Advance or Pro

### 2. The Performer (表演型創作者)
*Makes: Entertainment, comedy, music, performance*
- **Identity need:** On camera they're larger than life. Off camera, who are they?
- **Common pain:** "People know the character. Not the person playing it."
- **Design direction:** Cinematic. High contrast. Motion-rich. Personality-driven.
- **Example sections:** On Stage / Off Stage / The Real [Name]
- **Best tier:** Pro

### 3. The Maker (創作型創作者)
*Makes: Art, photography, design, crafts*
- **Identity need:** The work should be seen — and the person behind it understood.
- **Common pain:** "People see my work. They don't see the hands that made it."
- **Design direction:** Visual-first. Minimal text. Gallery-like. Process documentation.
- **Example sections:** Gallery / Process / Materials / Studio
- **Best tier:** Advance

### 4. The Builder (創業型創作者)
*Makes: Products, startups, businesses, communities*
- **Identity need:** The person is the brand. Investors, customers, and partners need to trust them.
- **Common pain:** "People see the product. Not the founder."
- **Design direction:** Clean. Professional. Story-driven. Vision-forward.
- **Example sections:** Vision / Story / Work / Values / Contact
- **Best tier:** Pro

### 5. The Athlete (運動型創作者)
*Makes: Sports content, training, competition, analysis*
- **Identity need:** Performance speaks. But there's more to them than stats.
- **Common pain:** "People see the highlights. Not the grind."
- **Design direction:** Cinematic dark. Timeline. Journey-heavy. Data-driven.
- **Example sections:** Journey / Training / Philosophy / Numbers
- **Best tier:** Pro

### 6. The Daily (生活型創作者)
*Makes: Vlogs, lifestyle, daily content*
- **Identity need:** Their life is their content. But their life is also real.
- **Common pain:** "People think they know my life. They know the edited version."
- **Design direction:** Warm. Intimate. Photography-heavy. Personal.
- **Example sections:** A Day With Me / Things I Love / Letters to Myself
- **Best tier:** Sample or Advance

---

## Using Personas

### In Discovery
Use the persona as a starting point for questions. The Storyteller needs different questions than the Athlete.

### In Design
The persona suggests a design direction — but never dictates it. The interview overrides the persona. Always.

### In Marketing
Create content that speaks to each persona's specific pain. "You're more than your highlights" speaks to the Athlete. "You're more than your analysis" speaks to the Storyteller.

---

*Personas are maps. The person is the territory. Always trust the territory.*
