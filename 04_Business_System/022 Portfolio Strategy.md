# 022 — Portfolio Strategy

## Your Portfolio Is Not a Gallery. It's a Conversion Engine.

Every project in the portfolio should make someone think: *"I want that feeling."*

---

## Portfolio Structure (on WKALAN site)

```
/work
  ├── Featured Case Study (full story, deep)
  ├── Case Study 2
  ├── Case Study 3
  └── All Projects (grid, filterable)
```

### Featured: Full case study
- Story arc: Before → Discovery → Direction → Identity → Response
- 1000+ words. Deep. The one you want everyone to read.

### Standard: Condensed case study
- Person + Keywords + 3 screenshots + Client quote
- 300 words. Quick. Impressive. Scannable.

### Grid: All projects
- Thumbnail + Name + Type (Creator / Brand / Startup)
- Filterable. Sortable. "50+ identities designed."

---

## Portfolio Rules

### 1. Quality over quantity
10 case studies is better than 50 screenshots. Show fewer projects. Go deeper on each one.

### 2. Lead with the person
Every project title should be a person's name. Not "Fashion Brand Website." But "NKSEN — A Brand That Feels Like Linen."

### 3. Show the process
Don't just show the final screenshot. Show the interview notes. The keyword extraction. The moodboard. The before → after. Let people see how understanding becomes design.

### 4. Include the client's voice
A quote from the client is worth 10 paragraphs of your own writing. *"I've never been understood like this."* — That sells harder than any design rationale.

### 5. Update every 3 months
The portfolio is a living document. Every quarter: add the best recent projects, remove the weakest old ones.

---

## Portfolio Goals

| Metric | Year 1 Target |
|--------|---------------|
| Published case studies | 10 |
| Featured (full story) | 3 |
| Portfolio → inquiry conversion | 3–5% |
| Average time on case study page | 3+ minutes |

---

*Your portfolio is not what you've made. It's what someone feels when they see what you've made.*
