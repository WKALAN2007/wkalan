# 005 — Client Journey

## Overview

Every WKALAN client travels the same path:

```
Discover → Inquire → Discover → Design → Build → Launch → Reflect
```

Each phase has a goal, a process, and a quality gate. Nothing skips. Nothing rushes.

---

## Phase 1: Discover (Client finds WKALAN)

**Goal:** They encounter WKALAN and think: *"This is different."*

**Channels:**
- WKALAN website
- Bilibili / Xiaohongshu / social media
- Friend referral
- Creator community

**What they see:**
- WKALAN philosophy (品味人生)
- Case studies (real people, real stories)
- Clear three-tier pricing
- "This isn't a web design studio. This is something else."

**Quality Gate:** They reach out. DM. Email. Contact form. First contact.

---

## Phase 2: Inquire (First contact)

**Goal:** Qualify the client. Set expectations.

**WKALAN response (within 24 hours):**
> "Hi [Name] — thanks for reaching out. WKALAN is a digital identity studio. We don't build websites — we design spaces where people can actually know you.
>
> Our process starts with a free 30-minute call. No pitch. No pressure. We just want to understand what you're looking for — and whether we're the right fit.
>
> Here's a link to book: [Calendly link]
>
> Talk soon,
> Alan"

**Quality Gate:** They book the call.

---

## Phase 3: Discover (The Deep Interview)

**Goal:** Understand this person more deeply than anyone has understood them online.

This is the WKALAN moat. See `007 Creator Interview Framework.md` for the full question bank.

**Structure:**
- 1–3 sessions, 60 minutes each (depending on tier)
- Recorded (with permission)
- Not a questionnaire. A conversation.

**Quality Gate:** At the end, they've said something they've never told anyone about themselves online.

---

## Phase 4: Design (From understanding to direction)

**Goal:** Translate understanding into visual direction.

**Process:**
1. Transcribe the interview. Extract keywords.
2. Find 3–5 words that are *uniquely theirs.*
3. Create a moodboard that reflects *them*, not trends.
4. Choose type, color, spacing based on their keywords.
5. Present the direction: "Here's what we heard. Here's what it means visually."

**Deliverable:** One-page design direction document. Keywords → Mood → Color → Type → Layout direction.

**Quality Gate:** Client reads it and says: *"How did you know that about me?"*

---

## Phase 5: Build (Making it real)

**Goal:** Translate design direction into a working digital identity.

**Process:**
- Day 1–3: Core structure (pages, sections, layouts)
- Day 4–6: Polish (animations, responsive, performance)
- Day 7–8: Content integration (client provides text and images)
- Daily updates: Screenshot or preview link every day

**Quality Gate:** It works on mobile. It loads fast. It feels like the person.

---

## Phase 6: Launch (Going live)

**Goal:** The digital identity is born.

**Process:**
1. Final review against `024 Quality Checklist.md`
2. Client signs off
3. Deploy to production
4. Client announces to their audience
5. WKALAN shares (with permission)

**Quality Gate:** The client's audience responds with: *"I never knew this about you."*

---

## Phase 7: Reflect (Case study)

**Goal:** Turn the project into proof.

**Process:**
1. Gather: Before screenshots, interview notes, design decisions
2. Write: The story of the transformation
3. Publish: On WKALAN site (with client permission)

**Quality Gate:** The case study makes someone else want this experience.
