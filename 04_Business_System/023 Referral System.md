# 023 — Referral System

## The Best Marketing Is a Client Who Felt Understood.

WKALAN doesn't need ads. It needs clients who can't stop talking about what it felt like to be understood.

---

## Referral Philosophy

**Don't ask for referrals. Earn them.**

A client who was truly understood will tell people. Your job is to make it easy for them — not to pressure them.

---

## When Referrals Happen Naturally

| Moment | What They Say |
|--------|--------------|
| After the interview | "I've never been asked questions like that." |
| At design presentation | "How did you know that about me?" |
| On launch day | "This is me. Finally." |
| One week later | "My friends keep saying 'this is SO you.'" |
| When a friend launches a channel | "You need to talk to Alan." |

---

## Making Referrals Easy

### 1. The Share-Ready Moment
On launch day, send the client:
- 3 screenshots of their site (optimized for social)
- A suggested caption: "Finally — a website that feels like me. Thanks @WKALAN."
- A direct link to WKALAN's contact page

Don't ask them to post. Just make it easy if they want to.

### 2. The "Tell a Friend" Email
One month after launch:
> "Hi [Name] — hope the site's feeling like home. Quick ask: if you know another creator who's tired of being reduced to numbers, I'd love an intro. No pressure at all. Just if it comes up naturally."

Light. Genuine. No guilt.

### 3. The Referral Discount (Future)
When WKALAN is established:
> "Refer a client, get 20% off your next project or a free annual maintenance."

Not for Year 1. Revenue is too important.
