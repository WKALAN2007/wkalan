# 025 — Marketing Strategy

## WKALAN Doesn't Market. WKALAN Is Marketed.

Every case study. Every interview. Every client who feels understood — these are the marketing. We don't run ads. We run on proof.

---

## Marketing Channels

### Primary: Case Studies (Earned)
The WKALAN site itself is the #1 marketing channel. Every case study is a landing page for a specific creator type.

### Secondary: Social Content (Owned)
| Platform | Content Type | Frequency |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Bilibili** | Behind-the-scenes of projects. Creator transformation stories. | 1–2×/month |
| **Xiaohongshu** | Before → After visuals. Design philosophy carousels. | 2–3×/month |
| **WeChat** | Long-form: "What I learned from understanding 50 creators." | 1×/month |

### Tertiary: Referral (Earned)
See `023 Referral System.md`. The flywheel.

### Future: Content Marketing
- **WKALAN Journal** — Essays on design, identity, and the creator economy. SEO-optimized.
- **Creator Spotlight** — Short interviews with clients. Their story, not our design.
- **Design Deep Dives** — "Why we chose this typeface for this person."

## Marketing Rules

### 1. Never sell. Show.
Don't post "Hire us." Post "Look what happened when we understood this person."

### 2. Lead with philosophy
Before someone sees the work, they should understand what we believe. Philosophy attracts the right clients. Portfolio attracts everyone.

### 3. One platform at a time
Year 1: Bilibili + Xiaohongshu. Master them. Then expand.

### 4. Repurpose everything
One case study becomes: a tweet thread, a carousel post, a Bilibili video, a newsletter edition. Create once. Distribute everywhere.
