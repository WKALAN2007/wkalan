# 021 — Case Study Framework

## A Case Study Is Not a Portfolio Entry.

It's a story. A transformation. Proof that the methodology works.

---

## The Structure

Every WKALAN case study follows the same narrative arc:

### 1. The Person (Before)
**Who were they before WKALAN?**

- Name. What they do. Platform. Following.
- Their online presence: a screenshot of their Bilibili page, their Instagram, their old website.
- The gap: what was missing between their content and who they really are.
- A quote from them about how they felt: *"I have 300K followers. But does anyone actually know me?"*

### 2. The Discovery (What We Found)
**What did we learn when we listened?**

- Key moments from the interview. Things they'd never said out loud before.
- The 3 keywords we extracted. Why those three.
- A short excerpt from the transcript (with permission). Something real.

### 3. The Direction (Why We Designed It This Way)
**How did understanding become design?**

- The design decisions traced back to the keywords.
- "We chose [color] because [what they told us]."
- "We used [layout] because [how they see the world]."
- Show the moodboard. Show the thinking.

### 4. The Identity (The Result)
**What did we build?**

- Screenshots. Full-page. Key sections.
- Live link to the site.
- Side-by-side: Before → After.
- The site in motion (GIF or video of scroll experience).

### 5. The Response (The Impact)
**What changed for them?**

- Quote from the client: *"When I opened the site, I cried."*
- Audience response: *"Comments, DMs, reactions from their community."*
- Tangible outcome (if any): *"Booked 3 brand deals. First time anyone understood my value."*

---

## Case Study Rules

### 1. Permission first
Never publish without explicit client consent. Pro tier includes permission by default. For others, ask.

### 2. Show the person, not the pixels
A case study with 10 screenshots and no story is a portfolio entry. We tell stories.

### 3. Be specific about decisions
Not "we chose a warm palette." But "we chose warm amber tones because he described his childhood kitchen as 'always golden in the afternoon.'"

### 4. Include the client's voice
Direct quotes. Interview excerpts. Let them tell their own story.

### 5. Every case study improves the system
After publishing, review: What did we learn? What should we add to the playbook? What question should we add to the interview framework?
