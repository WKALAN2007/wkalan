North Brand Guidelines

Version 1.0 · January 2025

Our Brand

North is a memory technology company. We build tools that help families preserve their stories across generations. Our brand reflects what we stand for: permanence, warmth, and trust. Every visual and verbal choice should feel like it was made by someone who cares deeply about what they are building.

Logo

Primary Wordmark

The North wordmark is set in Instrument Serif — a typeface chosen for its timeless, literary quality. The wordmark should never be stretched, skewed, or recolored outside the approved palette.

Clear Space

Always maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the capital "N". No other elements should enter this zone.

Minimum Size

Print: 25mm wide. Digital: 120px wide. Never use the logo smaller than these dimensions.

Incorrect Usage

  • Do not rotate or angle the logo
  • Do not add drop shadows or effects
  • Do not place on busy backgrounds
  • Do not change the logo colors

Typography

Instrument Serif — Headings

Geist Sans — Body text. Clean, modern, highly readable at small sizes. Used for all body copy, UI elements, and captions.

Geist Mono — Code & data. Used for technical specifications, file names, and data displays.

Type Scale

H1: Instrument Serif, 52px
H2: Instrument Serif, 36px
H3: Instrument Serif, 24px
Body: Geist, 16px / line-height 1.6
Caption: Geist, 12px / letter-spacing 0.08em

Brand Voice

"We write like we speak. Thoughtfully. Honestly. Like someone who genuinely cares about what they're saying."

We Are

Warm but not sentimental. Confident but not boastful. Clear but not cold. Human but not casual.

We Are Not

Corporate. Hype-driven. Jargon-heavy. Cute. Urgent for urgency's sake.

Examples

Yes: "Your stories deserve to be remembered."
No: "Revolutionary AI-powered memory preservation solution."

Yes: "We built this for our families. Now we're building it for yours."
No: "Leveraging cutting-edge technology to disrupt the legacy space."

Imagery

Photography should feel warm, honest, and intimate. Use natural light whenever possible. Avoid stock photography that feels staged or corporate. Every image should tell a real human story.

  • Prefer candid over posed
  • Natural light over studio lighting
  • Warm tones over cool tones
  • Real environments over seamless backdrops
