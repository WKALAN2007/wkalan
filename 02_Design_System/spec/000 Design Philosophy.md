# 000 — Design Philosophy

## Philosophy

WKALAN exists because the internet reduces people to numbers. Followers. Views. Engagement rates.

Design is how we reverse that.

**We design spaces where real people can be found.**

Every WKALAN project — every pixel, every word, every animation — exists to serve one goal: help someone be understood. Not watched. Not measured. Understood.

---

## Principles

### Good design is never random.
Every decision has a reason. Every color traces back to a word spoken in an interview. Every animation serves the narrative. Nothing is decoration.

### If a design decision cannot be explained, it should not exist.
This is the WKALAN test. Before any element ships, ask: *"Why is this here?"* If the answer is "it looks good" — remove it. If the answer connects to the person's story — keep it.

### Simplicity reveals meaning.
Complexity hides. Simplicity exposes. The less there is on a page, the more weight each remaining element carries. We design through subtraction — removing everything that doesn't belong to the person.

### Design should help people understand people.
Not impress. Not decorate. Not "delight." Understand. A WKALAN site is successful when a stranger leaves feeling like they met someone.

### We design for stories, not for trends.
Trends expire in 18 months. Stories last. We don't chase glassmorphism, brutalism, or the aesthetic of the moment. We chase what is true about the person — and true things don't date.

---

## Specification

### The WKALAN Design Test
Every design decision must pass three questions:

1. **Why?** — What in the client's story justifies this decision?
2. **What happens if we remove it?** — If nothing breaks, remove it.
3. **Would it still work in 5 years?** — If trends would kill it, rethink it.

### What We Never Do
- Animate for attention. Motion serves understanding, not spectacle.
- Decorate with color. Color directs attention, never fills space.
- Add elements without reasons. "It felt empty" is not a reason.
- Use AI to generate final design. AI assists. Humans decide.
- Chase platform conventions. We define our own.

### What We Always Do
- Start with listening. Hours of conversation before any pixel.
- Design from keywords. Three words extracted from the interview define everything.
- Use one animation curve. Consistency is invisible craftsmanship.
- Leave generous space. Space is respect for the content and the reader.

## Examples

### ✅ Correct
> "We chose this warm amber gradient because he described his childhood kitchen as 'always golden in the afternoon.' The gradient direction follows the window light he mentioned."

**Why it passes:** Specific. Traced to the person's story. Measurable. Defendable.

### ❌ Incorrect
> "We used a gradient because it looks modern and adds visual interest."

**Why it fails:** Generic. Not connected to the person. "Looks modern" expires. "Visual interest" means nothing.

### ✅ Correct
> "This section has no animation. The story is heavy enough. Motion would distract from the words."

**Why it passes:** Intentional stillness. The absence of motion is a decision, not an oversight.

### ❌ Incorrect
> "We added a fade-in because every section should have animation."

**Why it fails:** Automatic thinking. "Every section should have X" is a rule without a reason.
