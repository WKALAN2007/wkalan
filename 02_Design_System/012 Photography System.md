# 012 — Photography System

## Philosophy

**A photograph says: "This person exists. This really happened."**

Photography is the most important visual element in a WKALAN project. More than color. More than typography. More than motion. A photograph is evidence — it carries a weight that no designed element can match.

WKALAN photography direction: **documentary, not advertising.**

---

## Photography Principles

### 1. Natural Light
No studio lighting. No ring lights. No flash. Light from a window. Light from the sun. Light that existed in the moment.

Why: Natural light is honest. Studio light is controlled. We're not interested in control. We're interested in truth.

### 2. Unposed When Possible
The best photographs are the ones where someone forgot the camera was there. Laughing. Thinking. Looking away. The moments between poses.

Why: Posed photographs say "I'm performing." Unposed photographs say "I'm here."

### 3. Context Matters
Don't shoot people against white backgrounds. Shoot them in their space. Their desk. Their kitchen. Their studio. The place where they actually live and work.

Why: A person is inseparable from their environment. The background tells as much story as the face.

### 4. Imperfection Is Character
Blur. Grain. Awkward framing. These are not flaws. They're proof that a human took this photograph.

Why: Perfection is algorithmic. Imperfection is human.

### 5. Consistency Within a Project
One project = one visual language for photography. Same color temperature. Same level of contrast. Same depth of field.

Why: Inconsistent photography feels like a mood board. Consistent photography feels like a story.

---

## Photography by Project Type

### Personal Creators
- **Subject:** The person. Their face. Their hands. Their space.
- **Style:** Intimate. Close. Warm tones.
- **Ratio:** 3:4 portrait, 1:1 square
- **Reference:** Annie Leibovitz's intimate portraits, not her celebrity work

### Fashion / Lifestyle Brands
- **Subject:** The product in context. Models in natural movement.
- **Style:** Editorial. Slightly desaturated. High depth of field.
- **Ratio:** 3:4 portrait, 16:9 landscape
- **Reference:** COS, Acne Studios, Estudio Niksen

### Cinematic / High-Impact
- **Subject:** The person in motion. Energy. Intensity.
- **Style:** High contrast. Dramatic lighting. Deep shadows.
- **Ratio:** 16:9, 21:9 cinematic
- **Reference:** Nike campaigns, LeBron James .com

## Image Treatment

| Treatment | Usage |
|-----------|-------|
| Full-bleed | Hero images — edge to edge, no borders |
| Contained with radius | Gallery images — `--radius-lg` |
| No border, no shadow | Always — images are not cards |
| Dark gradient overlay | When text overlays images — ensures readability |
| Desaturation (subtle) | Editorial style — -5% to -10% saturation |
| Black and white | Only when color doesn't serve the story |

---

## Photography Rules

### 1. Never use stock photography
Stock photography is the antithesis of WKALAN. We design for real people. The photographs must be of real people — the ones we're designing for.

### 2. Never use AI-generated imagery
AI images are not photographs. They are simulations. They have no history, no moment, no truth. WKALAN rejects them entirely.

### 3. One photographer per project
If we commission photography, one photographer shoots the entire project. Consistency of eye is more valuable than variety.

### 4. Alt text on every image
Every photograph must have descriptive alt text. Not "hero image." Not "photo of person." But: "WKALAN founder Alan standing in his studio, afternoon light from the window to his left."

---

*The best photograph is the one that makes you forget you're looking at a photograph — and remember you're looking at a person.*
