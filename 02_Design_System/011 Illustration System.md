# 011 — Illustration System

## Philosophy

**We don't illustrate. We photograph.**

WKALAN's default position on illustration: **don't.**

Illustration is a distinct craft. Unless we're working with a dedicated illustrator whose style aligns with the project, we don't add illustrations. Photography of real people, real spaces, real things is almost always more powerful.

---

## When Illustration Is Allowed

1. **The client is an illustrator.** Their digital identity should feature their work.
2. **Technical explanation.** A diagram or flow that's clearer as a simple drawing than as text.
3. **Cultural context.** An illustration style that carries specific meaning for the client's audience.
4. **Playful brand.** A client whose identity is inherently illustrated (children's content, comics, animation).

---

## Illustration Style (When Used)

If illustration is used, it follows these constraints:

- **Style:** Editorial line art or flat with texture — never corporate vector
- **Color:** Monochrome (ink black) or using the client's palette — never a rainbow
- **Complexity:** Simple enough to understand at a glance. No detail for detail's sake.
- **Integration:** Illustration sits alongside photography and typography — it doesn't dominate.

---

## The Hand-Drawn Exception

Estudio Niksen uses a single hand-drawn doodle in their newsletter section. It works because:
- It's the only illustration on the entire site
- It's clearly human — imperfect lines, personality
- It contrasts with the polished photography

This is the WKALAN way to use illustration: **one, human, earned.**

---

## What We Never Do

- **Stock illustration** — Never. Not even "premium" stock.
- **AI-generated illustration** — Never. Contradicts 品味人生.
- **Corporate vector art** — The blue-gradient-human-silhouette aesthetic. Instant rejection.
- **Icon overuse as illustration** — Icons are signposts. They're not a visual language.

---

*When in doubt: use a photograph. A real person is always more interesting than a drawing of one.*
